Pilot Regions

Seven diverse sites demonstrate how tailored nature‑based solutions transform landscapes and livelihoods.

Our Demonstration Sites

Click on a pilot card to read more about the local challenges, interventions and expected outcomes.

Menemen Plain (Türkiye)

A fertile plain in the Gediz Basin facing irregular rainfall, groundwater depletion and salinity risk.

Challenges:
  • Decreasing and irregular rainfall
  • Groundwater depletion and soil salinisation
  • Ecosystem degradation and soil erosion
Nature‑Based Solutions:
  • Riparian buffer strips
  • Contour terracing
  • Biochar application
  • Ecological pest management
Expected Contribution:

Improved water use efficiency, enhanced soil quality and habitat connectivity, and better monitoring and modelling capacities.

Amyntaio Basin (Greece)

A sensitive lake system in Western Macedonia with natural wastewater lagoons serving 14,000 residents.

Challenges:
  • Non‑compliance with EU wastewater directives
  • Ecological fragility of lakes
  • Climate‑driven yield fluctuations
Nature‑Based Solutions:
  • Lagoon restoration with algae‑based photobioreactors
  • In‑situ treatment barriers using zeolite and attapulgite
Expected Contribution:

Enhanced removal of nitrogen and phosphorus, improved water quality and low‑cost treatment models.

Trasimeno Lake (Italy)

An experimental olive grove in Umbria with high genetic diversity and important economic value.

Challenges:
  • Increasing drought and declining yields
  • Soil erosion and decreasing lake levels
  • Need for model validation
Nature‑Based Solutions:
  • Organic mulching and amendments
  • Cover cropping
  • Subsurface drip irrigation
  • Infiltration trenches
Expected Contribution:

Improved irrigation efficiency, reduced soil erosion, increased groundwater recharge and enhanced soil fertility.

Amman‑Zarqa Basin (Jordan)

A semi‑arid landscape in central Jordan combining urban and agricultural land uses.

Challenges:
  • Irregular rainfall and droughts
  • Soil erosion and overgrazing
  • Wastewater pollution and rapid urbanisation
Nature‑Based Solutions:
  • Terracing and stone bunds
  • Contour tillage/strip cropping
  • Agroforestry
  • Managed aquifer recharge
  • Constructed wetlands
  • Urban green spaces
Expected Contribution:

Reduced soil and water losses, increased groundwater recharge, improved biodiversity and better climate resilience.

Azilal & Tadla Region (Morocco)

A highland area with clayey‑silty soils supporting fruit trees, cereals and forage crops.

Challenges:
  • Limited water resources and poor irrigation infrastructure
  • Soil degradation and risk of salinisation
  • Smallholder farming and rural outmigration
Nature‑Based Solutions:
  • Water harvesting in small basins
  • Terrace farming with solar‑powered pumps
  • Artificial wetlands for wastewater treatment
  • Alternative drought‑resilient crops
Expected Contribution:

Reduced water scarcity, decreased soil erosion, diversified rural livelihoods and enhanced smallholder income.

Peri‑urban Barcelona (Spain)

A horticultural zone supplying fresh produce to Barcelona, characterised by intensive production and water scarcity.

Challenges:
  • Water scarcity and competition with urban supply
  • High levels of micropollutants
  • Low‑quality irrigation water
Nature‑Based Solutions:
  • Bio‑electrochemically assisted vertical‑flow wetlands
  • Horizontal‑flow root mat wetlands
Expected Contribution:

Improved irrigation water quality, reduced health and biodiversity risks, enhanced aquifer recharge and nutrient recovery.

Andalusia Region (Spain)

Spain’s largest agricultural area facing droughts, aquifer overexploitation and diffuse contamination from fertilisers and pesticides.

Challenges:
  • Droughts and water scarcity
  • Aquifer overexploitation
  • Diffuse contamination and high evaporation
Nature‑Based Solutions:
  • Hybrid constructed wetlands
  • Permeable reactive barriers
  • Soil treatment units
Expected Contribution:

Improved wastewater treatment and reuse for irrigation, enhanced groundwater recharge, reduced contaminants and increased water availability.
